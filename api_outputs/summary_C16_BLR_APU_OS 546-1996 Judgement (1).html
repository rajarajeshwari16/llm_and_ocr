Document Summary

Document Type

Court Judgment

Parties Involved

Plaintiffs: Sri. Mohan and Smt. Pushapa (children of Late Ramareddy from his wife Smt. Chikka Pillamma). Defendants: A large group including Smt. Chikka Pillamma (Defendant No. 1, wife of Late Ramareddy); the heirs of Sri. Srinivasareddy (son of Late Ramareddy from another wife, Muniyamma), including his wives Smt. Munirathnamma and Smt. Jayamma and their children; Sri. Venkataswamy Reddy (son of Late Ramareddy); Smt. Lakshmamma (daughter of Late Ramareddy); Sri. R.Gopala Reddy (claiming to be a son of Late Ramareddy); and subsequent purchasers of the properties including Sri. Srinivasareddy (Defendant No. 7), Smt. K.P.Dayanandini (Defendant No. 9), M/s Daya Associates (Defendant No. 12), and Azim Premji Trust (Defendant No. 14).

Property Details

The suit involves four properties referred to as 'suit schedule properties': Item No. 1: Land bearing Sy.No.47/1 measuring 3 acres 33 guntas in Boorkunte village. Item No. 2: A house property in Booragunte Village. Item No. 3: Agricultural land bearing Sy.No.65 measuring 3 acres 26 guntas. Item No. 4: Agricultural land bearing Sy.No.76/16 measuring 8 guntas.

Key Dates

Date of institution of suit: 04-11-1996; Date of pronouncement of Judgment: 24-04-2024; Sale Deed for Item No. 1 (to Defendant No. 7): 05.12.2005; Subsequent Sale Deed for Item No. 1: 02.04.2019; Sale Deed for Item No. 3: 14.09.2007; Gift Deed for Item No. 4: 25.09.2004; Sale Deed for Item No. 3 (to Azim Premji Trust): 12.10.2011; Death of Ramareddy: 17.10.1983.

Legal Terms

Partition, Declaration, Mesne Profits, Permanent Injunction, Hindu Undivided Joint family, Lis-pendense, Counter claim, Written statement, Propositus, Co-parceners, Occupancy right, Hakku pathra, Preliminary decree, Final decree, Bonafide purchasers, Deed of Relinquishment, Re-joinder, Examination in chief, Cross examination, Adverse inference, Metes and bounds, Order-20 Rule 12 of CPC, GPA (General Power of Attorney).

Summary

This document is a judgment in a long-standing civil suit (OS.No. 546/1996) concerning a family property dispute. The plaintiffs, Sri. Mohan and Smt. Pushapa, children of the late Ramareddy from his fourth wife, sued for partition of four properties, claiming them to be ancestral and part of a Hindu Undivided Joint Family. The defendants include other heirs of Ramareddy from a different wife, as well as subsequent purchasers of the disputed properties. The plaintiffs sought their legitimate share and a declaration that sale deeds executed during the litigation are void. The court, after examining extensive evidence and a complex history of prior litigation (including OS No.58/1986 and OS No.136/1987), determined that only suit schedule Item Nos. 1 (land Sy.No.47/1) and 2 (house property) were ancestral joint family properties. The court decreed the suit in part, granting the plaintiffs a 1/5th share each in these two properties. Crucially, it declared the sale deeds for Item No. 1 dated 05.12.2005 and 02.04.2019 to be null, void, and not binding on the plaintiffs' share, applying the principle of lis pendens. The suit regarding Item Nos. 3 and 4 was dismissed as they were found to be the self-acquired property of Defendant No. 1. The counter-claims of other legitimate heirs for their shares in Items 1 and 2 were allowed, while the claim of Defendant No. 8, who failed to prove he was a son of Ramareddy, was dismissed.
