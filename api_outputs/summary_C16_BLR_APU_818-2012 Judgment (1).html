Summary

This document is a judgement from the Court of IV Addl. Civil Judge & JMFC, Anekal, in the civil suit O.S.No.818/2012, dated July 26, 2022. The plaintiff, Azim Premji Trust, filed for a permanent injunction against six defendants, the family of the late Munivenkatappa. The Trust claimed absolute ownership and lawful possession of two land parcels, Sy. no. 72/1 and Sy. no. 72/3 in Gorgunte village, acquired via registered sale deeds in 2011. The suit was filed after the defendants allegedly attempted to trespass on the properties on October 15, 2012. During the proceedings, two defendants were placed ex-parte for non-appearance, while the other four appeared but failed to file a written statement or contest the plaintiff's evidence. The court, finding the plaintiff's oral and documentary evidence (including sale deeds Ex.P.1 and Ex.P.2) to be unchallenged, ruled in their favor. Citing legal precedent, the court drew an adverse inference against the non-contesting defendants. Consequently, the court decreed the suit, granting a permanent injunction that restrains the defendants and their representatives from interfering with the plaintiff's peaceful possession and enjoyment of the specified properties.
