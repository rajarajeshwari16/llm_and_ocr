Document Summary

Document Type

Complaint Response

Parties Involved

Complainant: Unnamed plot owner. Respondent: Azim Premchand University. Other mentioned parties: D.G.M. Vijit Mukherjee (Rohan Builders), Site Incharge Surendra Reddy (Shri Sai Stone), Chandrashekhar (Head of Azim Premchand University), Town and Country Planning, Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation, Kanhansaiya Panchayat, Forest Department.

Property Details

A private plot of land adjacent to the Azim Premchand University campus. The university's land was provided by the government following official measurement and demarcation. The dispute involves an alleged encroachment where an 8-inch thick, 12-15 feet high concrete wall was constructed, blocking access to the complainant's plot. A government drain at the triangular corner of the plot is also mentioned.

Key Dates

The complainant mentions meeting with site officials 'About 02-03 months ago' prior to the document's creation.

Legal Terms

measurement and demarcation, unauthorized encroachment, rightful possession, illegal act, notice, hearing, compensation for damages, illegal construction, government permissions

Summary

This document is a formal, point-by-point response from Azim Premchand University to a complaint filed by a private plot owner. The complainant alleges that the university committed 'unauthorized encroachment' by breaking their fence, destroying the entrance to their plot, and constructing a 12-15 foot high concrete wall that has deprived them of access, an act they claim was done without notice or a hearing. The complainant also claims the university illegally altered a government drain to create a path. The university categorically denies these allegations, stating that all its construction is confined to land legally provided by the government after official 'measurement and demarcation'. The university asserts it has obtained all necessary permissions from government bodies including Town and Country Planning, Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation, Kanhansaiya Panchayat, and the Forest Department, and therefore, no encroachment on private land has occurred. The university dismisses the complaint as irrelevant because all its actions are legally sanctioned, while the complainant seeks the removal of the 'illegal construction', restoration of access, and 'compensation for damages'.
