Document Summary
================

Document Type:
Point-wise Clarification in Response to a Complaint

Parties Involved:
Complainant: Unnamed plot owner. Respondent: Azim Premchand University. Mentioned Individuals/Entities: D.G.M. Vijit Mukherjee (Rohan Builders), Site Incharge Surendra Reddy (Shri Sai Stone), Chandrashekhar (head of Azim Premchand University). Government Bodies: Town and Country Planning, Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation, Kanhasaiya Panchayat, Forest Department.

Property Details:
The complainant's plot, which is adjacent to the university's construction site. The university's land, which was provided by the government for its operation.

Key Dates:
The complainant mentions meeting officials on-site 'about 02-03 months ago'.

Legal Terms:
unauthorized encroachment, lawful possession, illegal act, demarcation permission, damages/costs, illegal construction

Summary:
This document is a formal, point-by-point response from Azim Premchand University to a complaint filed by an adjacent plot owner. The complainant alleges that the university has committed unauthorized encroachment by breaking their fence and display board, destroying their access path, and constructing a 12-15 feet high concrete wall, thereby depriving them of access. The complainant also accuses the university of illegally tampering with a government drain. In its defense, the university categorically denies any unauthorized encroachment on private land. It asserts that all construction is being carried out legally on land measured and provided by the government, and that it has secured all necessary permissions from authorities like Town and Country Planning, Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation, Kanhasaiya Panchayat, and the Forest Department. The university dismisses the complainant's prior communication with construction personnel as irrelevant, maintaining that all work adheres to government rules. The complainant seeks the removal of the alleged illegal construction, the reopening of their access path, and compensation for damages, while the university's stance is that the complaint is baseless as its actions are fully sanctioned and lawful.
