Summary:
This document is the final judgment in a protracted civil suit (OS.No. 546/1996) concerning partition and declaration of rights over ancestral properties. The plaintiffs, Sri. Mohan and Smt. Pushapa, children of the late Ramareddy from his wife Chikka Pillamma, sued for their legitimate share in what they claimed were Hindu Undivided Joint Family properties. The defendants included Ramareddy's children from another wife, their legal heirs, and multiple subsequent purchasers of the disputed properties, including the Azim Premji Trust. The plaintiffs specifically sought to nullify sale deeds from 2005 and 2019 for Suit Item No. 1 (Sy.No.47/1), arguing they were executed without their consent and during the pendency of the suit (lis-pendens). The court partially decreed the suit, affirming that Suit Item Nos. 1 and 2 were indeed joint family properties, but Nos. 3 and 4 were not. Consequently, the court granted each plaintiff a 1/5th share in Item Nos. 1 and 2 and declared the contested sale deeds for Item No. 1 null and void as they pertained to the plaintiffs' shares. The counter-claim of Defendant No. 8 (Gopala Reddy) was dismissed as he failed to prove his lineage. A preliminary decree for partition was ordered, along with a separate enquiry for mesne profits.
