Property Details

The suit properties, referred to as schedule A and B, consist of two adjacent land parcels: Sy. No. 72/1, measuring 1 acre 35 guntas, and Sy. No. 72/3, measuring 21 guntas. These properties are situated at Gorgunte village, Sarjapura hobli, Anekal taluk. The plaintiff acquired these lands through registered sale deeds, and the katha is in the plaintiff's name. The historical ownership includes Chinnarayappa, his sons Pappaiah and Muniswamy @ Muniyappa, Mr. Anwar Khan, and Papaiaha.

Summary

This document is a judgment from the Court of Civil Judge in Anekal, dated 26-07-2022, concerning Original Suit No. 818/2012. The plaintiff, Azim Premji Trust, filed the suit seeking a permanent injunction against six defendants, all related to late Munivenkatappa, to protect its peaceful possession and enjoyment of two land parcels, Sy. No. 72/1 (1 acre 35 guntas) and Sy. No. 72/3 (21 guntas), located in Gorgunte village, Anekal taluk. The Trust asserted lawful ownership and continuous peaceful possession since acquiring the properties through registered sale deeds in 2011, tracing the title through earlier transactions back to 1967. The plaintiff alleged that the defendants, described as land grabbers, attempted to trespass on the property on 15.10.2012. Defendants 2 and 3 were declared ex-parte, while the remaining defendants, despite appearing through counsel, failed to file a written statement or contest the plaintiff's evidence. The court, finding the plaintiff's oral and documentary evidence unchallenged and sufficient to prove peaceful possession and enjoyment, ruled in favor of the Azim Premji Trust. Consequently, the suit was decreed with costs, and the defendants, along with their agents and associates, were permanently restrained from interfering or meddling with the plaintiff's peaceful possession and enjoyment of the schedule suit properties.
