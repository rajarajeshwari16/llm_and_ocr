Summary

This judgment, delivered by the Court of the IV Additional Civil Judge and JMFC at Anekal, resolves a legal dispute (O.S. No. 818/2012) regarding a suit for permanent injunction filed by the Azim Premji Trust. The plaintiff sought to protect its lawful possession and ownership of two adjacent land parcels in Boorgunte village, identified as Survey No. 72/1 and 72/3, totaling 2 acres and 16 guntas. The plaintiff established its title through a series of registered sale deeds dating back to 1967, culminating in its own acquisition in 2011, supported by conversion orders and updated katha records. The defendants, who are the legal heirs of late Munivenkatappa, were accused of attempting to trespass and interfere with the property on October 15, 2012. Despite being served summons, the defendants failed to file written statements or effectively contest the evidence, leading the court to draw an adverse inference against them. Ultimately, the court ruled in favor of the plaintiff, granting a permanent injunction that restrains the defendants and their agents from interfering with the plaintiff's peaceful possession and enjoyment of the suit properties.
